Nutrition Problem Solving Challenge

by Name O. Person | December 2, 2012

To encourage students’ abilities to reason and solve math and science problems, SAMSEC is providing quarterly challenges drawn from interesting situations in San Antonio. In this challenge, students must draw pictures of a healthy day’s meals, including a breakfast, lunch, snack, and dinner. Determine what some combinations of lunches might be that you could choose, be sure to include one item from each food group.

When Will I Ever Use This?

by Name O. Person | December 2, 2012

Local businesses and organizations can provide specific examples and a context for math and science teachers to help answer the student's question: "When will I ever use this?" SAMSEC helps organize small "show-and-tell" lunches where organizations can invite about a dozen math and science teachers for a lunch and an overview of how math and science can be used in various setting.

Mission Statement

SAMSEC (San Antonio Math-Science Education Coalition) implements and influences city-wide efforts to recruit, certify, and retain science and mathematics teachers for San Antonio schools.

SAMSEC began as an informal group following up on discussions at a conference of Noyce funded institutions. It grew to encompass educators, business and community leaders, intent on recruiting, retaining, and renewing mathematics and science teachers for the area's schools. SAMSEC now pursues these goals as a task force of the BRIDGE Project (sponsored by UTSA) whose focus is to unify math support and promote a stronger foundation of math skills in the community.
